<p><p>The electrical room thermostats market includes millivolt, 24-volt, and line voltage thermostats, each serving specific heating systems. Millivolt thermostats operate without external power, often used in gas heaters. 24-volt thermostats require a lower voltage and are commonly used in HVAC systems for precise temperature control. Line voltage thermostats operate at higher voltages, typically for baseboard heaters or radiant systems. Each type offers distinct advantages in energy efficiency, compatibility, and application, catering to various residential and commercial needs.</p></p>
