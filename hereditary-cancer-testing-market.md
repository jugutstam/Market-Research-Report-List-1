<p><p>Hereditary cancer testing primarily involves two types: DNA testing and RNA testing. DNA testing identifies mutations in genes associated with increased cancer risk, providing insights into inherited conditions. This enables proactive healthcare decisions and risk management strategies. Conversely, RNA testing focuses on gene expression, helping to assess how genes are activated or silenced in cancer development. Together, these testing types empower patients and healthcare providers to understand cancer risks and formulate personalized prevention and treatment plans.</p></p>
