<p><p>The gene sequencing service market encompasses various technologies, including first-generation sequencing, second-generation sequencing (NGS), and third and fourth-generation sequencing. First-generation sequencing, like Sanger sequencing, offers high accuracy but low throughput. Second-generation sequencing revolutionizes the field with massively parallel processing, enabling rapid and cost-effective sequencing. Third-generation sequencing, such as single-molecule real-time (SMRT) sequencing, allows for longer read lengths and real-time analysis, while fourth-generation sequencing focuses on even more advanced techniques, enhancing speed and accuracy for complex genomic studies.</p></p>
