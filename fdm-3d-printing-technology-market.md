<p><p>Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) is a widely used 3D printing technology that involves extruding thermoplastic materials layer by layer to create three-dimensional objects. The process begins with the heating of a filament, which is then deposited through a nozzle according to digital designs. The demand for rapid prototyping and customized production is fueling this expansion. Moreover, advancements in materials and printing technologies are enhancing the quality and durability of printed objects, further broadening FDM's application scope.</p><p>Current trends indicate a surge in the adoption of FDM technology for educational purposes and small-scale manufacturing, with a growing emphasis on sustainable materials.
