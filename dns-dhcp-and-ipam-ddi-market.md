<p><p>DNS (Domain Name System), DHCP (Dynamic Host Configuration Protocol), and IPAM (IP Address Management) together form the foundation for effective network management known as DDI. DNS translates domain names into IP addresses, enabling users to access resources through user-friendly names. DHCP automates the assignment of IP addresses, reducing manual configuration efforts and potential errors. IPAM provides centralized management and tracking of IP address spaces, ensuring efficient use of IP resources within networks.</p><p>The DNS, DHCP, and IPAM (DDI) market is experiencing robust growth driven by the increasing demand for efficient network management solutions owing to the proliferation of connected devices and the expansion of cloud computing services. Additionally, with the adoption of IPv6 and the growing focus on cybersecurity, businesses are seeking integrated DDI solutions that enhance visibility and control.
