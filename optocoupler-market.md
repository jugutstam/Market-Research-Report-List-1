<p><p>An optocoupler, also known as an optical isolator, is an electronic component that transmits signals between two isolated circuits by using light waves to ensure electrical isolation. It primarily consists of a light-emitting diode (LED) and a photodetector, which are encapsulated in a single package. This technology is widely used in various applications, including industrial automation, telecommunications, power management, and consumer electronics, due to its ability to protect sensitive components from high voltages and noise.</p><p>The Optocoupler Market is anticipated to grow at a CAGR of 6% during the forecast period. <p><p>The optocoupler market is divided into two main types: non-linear and linear optocouplers. Non-linear optocouplers are primarily used for applications requiring on/off switching, such as in power supply circuits or signal isolation, offering digital signal transmission. In contrast, linear optocouplers are designed for linear signal transmission, providing precise analog signal isolation, essential in applications like audio processing and feedback control systems. Both types enhance system performance while ensuring electrical isolation in various electronic applications.</p></p>
