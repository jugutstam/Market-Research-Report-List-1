<p><p>The Clostridium difficile treatment market is differentiated into two main types based on antibiotic usage: narrow-spectrum and broad-spectrum antibiotics. Narrow-spectrum antibiotics target specific bacteria responsible for the infection, minimizing disruption to the overall microbiome and reducing the risk of recurrence. In contrast, broad-spectrum antibiotics affect a wider range of bacteria, which can lead to significant alterations in gut flora and a higher chance of C. difficile recurrence. Both strategies are critical in managing and treating C. difficile infections effectively.</p></p>
