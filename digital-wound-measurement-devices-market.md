<p><ul><li>Contact Type</li><li>Non-Contact Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Digital wound measurement devices are categorized into two main types: contact and non-contact. Contact type devices involve direct interaction with the wound, using tools like electronic calipers or handheld scanners to measure dimensions and area. Conversely, non-contact type devices use advanced technologies such as infrared or imaging sensors to assess wounds without physical contact. This differentiation allows for tailored wound assessment approaches, addressing specific patient needs while minimizing discomfort and the risk of infection associated with direct contact methods.</p></p>
